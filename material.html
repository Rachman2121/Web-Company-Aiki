Mendong or Fimbris Umbrellaris

Mendong (Fimbristylis umbellaris) or also called purun rat is a type of grass that lives in swamps, including members of the Cyperaceae tribe. This plant produces woven material, so it is cultivated in several areas

Mendong is widespread in Indonesia and is found in all regions. It grows in swampy areas and grass fields. Also in irrigated, rainfed, lowland rice fields, and tidal rice fields. Mendong grows well in areas with temperatures ranging from 25-27 degrees and sufficient light exposure. Mendong generally grows wild in wet areas, for example in rice fields with a soil pH range of 4.5-8 in lowland areas (more than 1000 meters above sea level).

Benefit

Mendong is planted like planting rice in a rice field, but it is maintained that the fields are always watery. Mendong clumps are sickled after 6-9 months. After being selected, the stems of the mendong were dried in the sun, sometimes rubbed and flattened first

Mendong of good quality, after drying to form sheets as wide as lk. 4 mm, flat, soft, and feels a bit chewy. These sheets are then woven to make mats and various other weaves. Now the mendong woven material is further processed to be used as room slippers, bags, box or tube containers, table decorations and others. The main products of Mendong are handicrafts such as mats, ropes, bags, and other woven items. Tasikmalaya makes mendong crafts as one of its creative industry commodities. The use of mendong as a raw material for handicrafts is based on the strong and elastic nature of the mendong plant so that it can be shaped and woven. For this mendong handicraft product, there is no specific standardization that regulates the quality of the product. In addition, Mendong is one of the members of the Cyperaceae family which is known as a metal hyper-accumulator plant that is tolerant of high environmental metal concentrations so that it is used as Phytoremediation. Currently, mendong fiber is being developed as a raw material for Microcrytalline Cellulose (MCC) which is used as a filler in medicine, stabilizer in the food industry, and composite material in the plastics industry. MCC from mendong is expected to replace MCC from wood, thereby reducing deforestation and being more environmentally friendly.
